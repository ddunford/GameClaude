# Guide — Concept Art & Previz method

> Read before starting any concept or previz work. The core idea: **concept art is a decision made visible, not a picture — its value is in the alternatives explored and the direction chosen, so you explore broad and cheap before you finish anything, and every image answers a build question and is judged on-pitch by someone who didn't draw it.**

## The two failures this prevents
1. **Rendering before exploring.** Falling in love with the first idea and polishing it to a shine before the space of options was searched — so the team builds the first thing thought of, not the best thing found. The cost of a rough thumbnail is minutes; the cost of a wrong direction discovered in 3D is weeks. Breadth first is the whole discipline.
2. **Art for art's sake.** A gorgeous frame that answers no question the build was asking — no callouts, no decision, no feed into the look-bible. It reads as productivity and produces nothing the next discipline can build to. Concept that doesn't de-risk a decision is decoration.

## PRINCIPLES
The craft rules that hold across every concept task. Sections below give the process; these say what good looks like.
- **Explore before you render — breadth first, finish last.** Ten rough options beat one polished frame, because the value is in the *choice* and you can't choose from one. Finish only after the direction is picked.
- **Silhouette and shape before detail.** A design reads as a flat black shape or it doesn't read at all. Explore in silhouette, resolve value next, add colour and detail last — the order the eye itself parses an image.
- **Every image answers a question.** A concept or reference states what it's *for* — this form language, this proportion, this material, this mood, this staging. An image with no callout is half-done (`guides/art-direction.md`: the design packet / callout sheet).
- **Name every source.** A reference with no attribution is half a reference; borrowed film/game/photo references are named so the next artist has the springboard (`guides/art-direction.md`).
- **Serve the build, not the portfolio.** Concept exists to make the next discipline's job right-first-time — cheaper massing for `level-designer`, a clear target for the look-bible, a staged moment for `creative-review`. Judge it by what it de-risks, not by how it looks framed.
- **On-pitch is the point.** Explore toward the vision's ranked emotional beats and away from its anti-goals (`creative-review` owns this call). A stunning frame that's off-pitch has explored the wrong space — competent, and wrong.
- **Fidelity matches the decision, not the ego.** A layout question needs a grey-box paint-over, not a rendered hero; a material question needs a tight callout, not a wide vista. Spend finish where the decision needs it and nowhere else.
- **Compositional theory lives in `art-direction.md`.** Value/notan, focal point, colour ratios (60/30/10), leading lines, negative space — apply them, link to them, never restate them here. This guide owns the *process*; that guide owns the *theory*.

## The process — thumbnails to a picked, rendered target

The pipeline, each stage cheaper to change than the next:
1. **Brief the question.** What decision does this concept exist to make? "What does the Neon Hub threshold feel like crossing?" "What's the shape language of the creator-tool props?" Concept with no question is skipped.
2. **Thumbnails — many, small, fast.** Tiny value/shape studies, a dozen or more, deliberately varied — not a dozen versions of one idea. The goal is *range*: prove the obvious idea against the alternatives. Kill your darlings here, where it's free.
3. **Silhouette pass.** Read the strongest thumbnails as flat black shapes. Do the key masses read, distinct and recognisable, in the world's shape language? A design that fails the flat-black test is cut before value or colour is spent on it.
4. **Value roughs.** Resolve the notan on the survivors — light/dark structure, focal contrast — in greyscale, before colour. Most composition problems are value problems and are cheapest to fix here (`guides/art-direction.md`).
5. **Variants on the survivors.** Take the two or three that hold and push variations — proportion, mood, material treatment — so the picked direction is chosen against near neighbours, not just against wild alternatives.
6. **Direction picked** by `art-director` (on-style) and `creative-review` (on-pitch, fresh). *Now*, and only now:
7. **Render the chosen direction** to the fidelity the decision needs — enough to lock the look-bible target, no more. A polished frame that arrives before the pick is finish spent on an unproven idea.

The discipline is that **finish is the last 10%**, applied once, to the winner. Time spent rendering a thumbnail is time stolen from exploring the range.

## Mood & callout sheets — the two boards

The reference artifacts that feed `art-director`'s look-bible (`guides/art-direction.md` owns their construction theory; here is the concept-side craft):
- **Mood board — the *feel*.** A curated wall of real places, films, photos answering one question: *what should this feel like?* It points at a direction; it is not a draft of the asset. Target a *place*, not an adjective — "a Lisbon side-street an hour before sunset", not "warm and inviting."
- **Callout / design sheet — the *specifics*.** Each image annotated with what it's *for*: this roofline's rhythm, that concrete's wear, this window's proportion. Each answers a form/proportion/material question, not just "looks nice."
- **Every source named** — the springboard is only a springboard if the next artist can find it.

## Previz — staging a space or moment before it's built

Concept's spatial arm — de-risking layout and staging cheaper than a blockout:
- **Paint-overs and grey-box overpaints.** Paint over a rough blockout or grey-box to test how a space *reads* — sightlines, framing, focal termination, scale — before `level-designer` commits massing or `environment-artist` dresses it. A paint-over that reveals a dead sightline saves a rebuild.
- **Staging a moment.** For a key beat (the door transition, a landmark reveal), block the composition, camera, and focal path so the moment is judged before it's built — feeding `level-designer`'s hero views and `creative-review`'s pitch call.
- **Previz is rough by contract.** Its job is the decision, not the finish; a polished previz is a contradiction — if it's polished, the decision it was meant to inform was already made.
- **Previz proposes; the spatial authority disposes.** `level-designer` owns the buildable spec (plan/elevation/metrics); previz feeds and de-risks it, never replaces it.

## Feeding the look-bible — where concept lands

Concept is upstream of, and subordinate to, the committed look-bible (`art-director`'s artifact):
- Concept **explores and proposes**; the look-bible **locks**. A picked direction becomes look-bible input (palette swatches, shape-language vocabulary, material logic, stylisation level, lighting-register targets), authored by `art-director`.
- Concept does **not** self-approve into the bible — the pick is `art-director` (on-style) + `creative-review` (on-pitch). This is doctrine 1 (build ≠ verify): the explorer doesn't decide the exploration was right.
- Once the bible is locked, concept's job on that subject is done; further work is execution (`environment-artist`, `lighting-artist`, `vfx-artist`, `ui-ux-designer`), not exploration. Re-opening a locked look needs a reason, not a redraw.

## QUALITY BAR
A concept deliverable is *good* when all of these hold — judged by a fresh pass (`art-director` on-style, `creative-review` on-pitch), never self-signed:
- **It's a range, not a frame.** Real breadth was explored — varied thumbnails, silhouette-tested, value-resolved — and the recommendation is a *choice* among alternatives, not a single idea rendered.
- **It answers its question.** The build decision it exists to make is named and made visible; callouts say what each image is for; every source is named.
- **Silhouettes read.** Key masses are distinct, recognisable flat-black shapes in the world's shape language (`guides/art-direction.md`).
- **Value structure holds.** Clear notan, focal contrast where the eye should go, no mid-tone soup — resolved in greyscale before colour.
- **On-pitch.** Serves the vision's ranked beats, avoids its anti-goals; targets a *place*, not an adjective. (`creative-review` makes this call.)
- **Fidelity fits the decision.** Finish is spent where the decision needs it and withheld elsewhere; nothing was rendered before the direction was picked.
- **It feeds forward.** The output is usable by the look-bible / `level-designer` / QA as a target — not a standalone picture.

## COMMON FAILURE MODES
- **Rendering before exploring.** One polished hero frame, no alternatives — the first idea, shined, not the best idea, found. → thumbnails first, many and varied; finish only the picked direction.
- **No callouts.** Pretty reference/concept with no annotation of what it's *for*, no named sources. → each image answers a form/proportion/material/mood question; name every source (`art-direction.md`).
- **Off-pitch.** Technically strong, emotionally wrong for *this* game — explored the wrong space. → target the vision's beats; `creative-review` judges pitch, fresh.
- **Art for art's sake.** A frame with no build question behind it; concept as decoration, not decision. → state the decision the concept makes before starting; judge by what it de-risks.
- **Over-finished previz.** A polished previz — finish spent where roughness was the point, implying a decision already made. → previz is rough by contract; the decision is the product.
- **Concept overstepping.** Treating a picked concept as the locked look, or previz as the buildable spec. → the look-bible is `art-director`'s; the spatial spec is `level-designer`'s; concept proposes, they dispose.
- **Restating theory.** Re-deriving value/colour/composition rules instead of linking `art-direction.md`. → apply and link; one fact, one home.

## CHECKLIST
**Before starting:**
- [ ] The build decision this concept exists to make is named.
- [ ] Reference gathered and **named**; the target is a *place*, not an adjective.
- [ ] The fidelity the decision actually needs is decided (thumbnail / value rough / previz overpaint / rendered) — not defaulted to "finished".

**During:**
- [ ] Breadth explored — many varied thumbnails, not variations on one idea.
- [ ] Silhouette-tested (flat-black read) before value; value-resolved (notan) before colour.
- [ ] Variants pushed on the survivors so the pick is against near neighbours.
- [ ] Each image carries a callout (what it's for); sources named.
- [ ] Composition/colour applied per `art-direction.md` — not re-derived here.
- [ ] Nothing rendered to finish before a direction is picked.

**Before recommending a direction:**
- [ ] Quality bar met; the deliverable is a *choice among alternatives*, not a single frame.
- [ ] Picked by a fresh pass — `art-director` (on-style) + `creative-review` (on-pitch) — never self-picked.
- [ ] The chosen direction handed forward as look-bible input / hero-view target / previz for the space — usable, not standalone.
- [ ] One-line recommendation to `art-director` + Directors; the owner decides on vision-level look — never a self-lock into the bible.
